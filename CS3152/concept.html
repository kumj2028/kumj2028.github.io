Concept & high-level pitch

Based on ConceptDocument – Final and earlier revisions.

High concept

Reflexio is a puzzle-platformer where Joey, a baby koala kidnapped from his home, must escape a hostile alien planet. The player uses a magical umbrella to reflect the world about an axis, reconfiguring walls, floors, and hazards to open paths and survive.

Core fantasy

The core fantasy is clever spatial manipulation: by flipping the world across a mirror line, the player feels like they are “bending” the environment to their will. Reflection lets Joey bypass enemies, turn gaps into bridges, and convert dead-ends into new routes.

Unique selling points

  • Reflection mechanic: reflecting the entire level about a movable axis, instead of simply flipping the player character.
  • Reflectable vs. non-reflectable objects: certain blocks and hazards respond to reflection, while others stay fixed, creating interesting constraints.
  • Short, focused puzzles: small levels with low punishment for failure encourage experimentation and learning.

Tone & audience

The protagonist is cute and approachable, but the puzzles ask for real spatial reasoning. The target audience is players who enjoy brain-teasers and platformers that reward careful planning rather than pure reflexes.
