Playtesting & iteration

How Reflexio was evaluated and refined over the semester.

Test script focus

The playtest script targets three main questions:

  • Understanding reflection: can players grasp the reflection mechanic without heavy visual aids?
  • Controls: do movement and axis control feel responsive and not clunky?
  • Clarity: are objectives (reach the door, avoid spikes, collect zipper) obvious?

The script instructs players to think aloud while playing and uses a fixed control scheme (WASD/X to move the reflection line, R to reflect, arrow keys to move and jump).

Facilitators observe how quickly players discover the reflection mechanic, whether they can predict the outcome of a reflection, and where confusion or frustration arises.

Two-week milestone reports

The two-week reports document progress from early prototypes to final release:

  • Report 1 – Gameplay Prototype: building and validating the core reflection mechanic.
  • Report 2 – Technical Prototype: stabilizing the engine and level pipeline.
  • Report 3 – Alpha Release: first full slice of gameplay with multiple levels.
  • Report 4 – Beta Release: content complete, focusing on polish and bug fixing.
  • Report 5 – Final Release: final tuning and reflection on what worked and what didn’t.

Together, they show how playtesting feedback and technical constraints shaped the final game.
