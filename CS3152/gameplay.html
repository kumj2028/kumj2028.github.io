Gameplay specification

Based on the revised Gameplay Specification and related docs.

Core loop

Each level is a compact puzzle. The player navigates Joey through platforms and hazards, manipulating a reflection axis to rearrange the level. The loop is:

  • Survey the level and identify the exit and key obstacles.
  • Move the reflection axis into a useful position.
  • Trigger reflection to transform the layout.
  • Platform through the new configuration toward the exit door.
  • If Joey dies, restart quickly and try a new plan.

Controls (from gameplay & test script)

  • Arrow keys: move and jump Joey.
  • W/A/S/D/X: move the line of reflection around the level (exact mapping documented in the implementation).
  • R: trigger reflection using the current axis.

Reflection axis

The player can position the reflection axis and then flip the world about that line. Depending on the level, the axis may be horizontal or vertical; placing it cleverly is essential to turning gaps into bridges, dodging enemies, or making platforms reachable.

Objects & hazards

  • Reflectable blocks: move when the world is reflected, allowing them to become floors, walls, or stepping stones.
  • Non-reflectable blocks: fixed geometry that anchors the puzzle and constrains solutions.
  • Spikes & pits: kill Joey on contact.
  • Zippers / keys: collectibles that open the exit door once obtained.

Difficulty & progression

Early levels teach movement and basic reflection in safe contexts. Later levels combine multiple reflection steps, moving axes, and timing hazards to require deeper planning. Failure is frequent but low-friction thanks to quick restarts and short stages.
