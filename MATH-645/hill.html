Classical Cipher Tool

Hill Cipher (2×2)

A matrix-based cipher using linear algebra over \(\mathbb{Z}_{26}\).

Use this page to encrypt with a 2×2 Hill cipher and explore a known-plaintext attack. See how short blocks of text become vectors, how keys become matrices, and how solving for the key reveals the underlying structure.

Encrypt

Turn plaintext into ciphertext using a 2×2 key matrix.

Non-letter characters are typically ignored or stripped before the Hill cipher is applied.

The four letters form a 2×2 matrix by rows

Decrypt (Known-Plaintext Attack)

Recover a 2×2 key matrix from a short known plaintext–ciphertext pair, then use it to decrypt a longer message.

To break the Hill cipher (2×2), you need at least two plaintext blocks (4 letters) and their corresponding ciphertext blocks. This gives you enough equations to solve for the key matrix.

You can paste the key recovered above or try a different plausible key.
