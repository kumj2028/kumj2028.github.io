MATH-645: A Survey of Mathematical Problems

This page currently has the final project for the course, which explores the mathematics behind classical cryptographic ciphers.

There are interactive applications for classical cryptography: Caesar, Substitution, Vigenère, and Hill ciphers. These tools are meant for a practical demonstration of the concepts from my paper.

Overview

What lives in this MATH-645 space.

The focus of these pages is on classical ciphers: how they work, how to implement them, and how to break them using frequency analysis and other techniques. The pages below provide implementations, small experiments, and places to play with your own messages.

These tools are primarily for demonstration and teaching; they are not intended for modern secure communication.
