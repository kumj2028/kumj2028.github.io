MATH-656: Cryptography & Ciphers

Implementation and cryptanalysis of classic ciphers.

This site hosts interactive demos and notes for classical cryptography: Caesar, Vigenère, Hill, substitution ciphers, and more. Many of the ideas here are drawn from my paper on classical cipher implementation and cryptanalysis.

Read the paper »

Overview

What lives in this MATH-656 space.

The focus of this course segment is on classical ciphers: how they work, how to implement them, and how to break them using frequency analysis and other techniques. The pages below provide implementations, small experiments, and places to play with your own messages.

These tools are primarily for demonstration and teaching; they are not intended for modern secure communication.
