Improving Thinking Quality: 15 Guidelines

  1. State thinking expectations explicitly and often.
  2. Provide structured, repeated practice with feedback.
  3. Sequence challenges to match developmental levels.
  4. Align objectives and assessments to higher-order skills.
  5. Use authentic, open-ended problems.
  6. Build reflection and metacognition into activities.
  7. Design variety to reach different learning preferences.
  8. Don’t teach only to “students like you.”
  9. Prioritize threshold concepts over exhaustive coverage.
  10. Normalize discomfort with complexity.
  11. Model productive efficiency without shortcutting thought.
  12. Have students declare intended depth/approach to tasks.
  13. Address emotions; leverage motivation and curiosity.
  14. Continuously ask, “What do you think—and why?”
  15. Close the loop with timely, actionable feedback.
